# The Subtle Parts - Brand Context

## Brand Overview

The Subtle Parts is a handmade jewelry brand with a voice that is grounded, warm, and craft-focused — never salesy, never lofty. Think: relaxed, handmade by someone who loves the process.

## Brand Voice & Tone

Your brand tone is **approachable**, with a clear focus on using **persuasive, intentional language** that resonates with your audience. It's all about showing the handcrafted, unique aesthetic of your jewelry, while keeping the descriptions **practical, relatable, and easy to understand**. You avoid overly poetic or flowery language, instead focusing on **concise but vivid descriptions** that highlight the craftsmanship, versatility, and style of each piece.

### Voice Principles

- **Speak naturally**, as if explaining the product to a friend
- Highlight the **aesthetic result** of each piece, without over-embellishment or trite phrases
- Use **SEO-focused keywords thoughtfully** to attract the right customers while sounding natural and not forced
- Balance **practical and aspirational**, reflecting the handmade, artistic nature of your jewelry while ensuring it feels relatable, wearable, and giftable
- Emphasize **authenticity and connection** with your audience

### Language to Avoid

**Trite phrases that should never appear:**

- "full of movement"
- "moves with you"
- "elevate your style"
- "timeless elegance"
- "perfect for any occasion"
- "add a touch of"
- "statement-making"
- Overly poetic or flowery sales language

**Instead, use:**

- Specific material descriptions (hammered copper, wire-wrapped, oxidized silver)
- Concrete aesthetic references (70s festival, minimal modernist, art deco inspired)
- Texture and shape descriptors (geometric fringe, organic curves, layered loops)

## Target Audience

The Subtle Parts' target audience is **modern contemporary women who embrace an eclectic approach to fashion**—they value individuality and want their style to reflect their personality and values.

### Audience Characteristics

- **Style-conscious creatives** who are confident and appreciate the balance of effortless beauty and bold design
- **Ethically minded** consumers who care deeply about sustainable and responsible practices
- **Artisan appreciators** drawn to handmade jewelry that stands out from mass-produced pieces
- **Seekers of meaning** who want statement pieces that feel personal and carry significance
- **Affordable luxury shoppers** who value quality artisan-made items within a reasonable price range
- **Boho aesthetic lovers** who gravitate toward the carefree "It girl" energy that mixes handmade charm with modern elegance
- **Natural material enthusiasts** attracted to copper, silver, and earthy stones
- **Vibrant lifestyle seekers** who resonate with tropical, artistic energy and modern rustic aesthetics

### What They Want

- Jewelry that reflects their personality and values
- Unique pieces that tell a story
- Quality craftsmanship that supports ethical practices
- Designs that complement their eclectic, individual style
- The spirit of freedom, emotion, and feminine connection to nature

## Materials & Craft Terminology

Use accurate, specific terms when describing pieces:

- Hammered copper/silver/brass
- Wire-wrapped
- Oxidized/patinated
- Lightweight dangle
- Geometric fringe
- Textured
- Hand-forged
- Artisan-made
- Mixed metal
- Raw/natural stone
- Minimalist/maximalist design

## Aesthetic References

When describing style influences, use grounded, clear language like the following but not limited to:

- 70s festival meets contemporary artisan
- Minimal modernist
- Art deco inspired
- 90s grunge elegance
- Southwestern modern
- Organic bohemian
- Industrial chic
- Vintage-inspired contemporary
