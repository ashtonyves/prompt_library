# The Subtle Parts Brand Voice

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive brand voice guidelines and copywriting frameworks for The Subtle Parts, a Miami-based handmade copper jewelry brand. Use this skill to create marketing copy that captures the brand's warm, unfussy, and craft-focused voice across all content types.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill whenever you need to write copy for The Subtle Parts, including:
- Product descriptions (Etsy, Shopify)
- Email newsletters and campaigns
- Instagram posts and captions
- Pinterest pins
- Facebook posts
- Ads (social media, display)
- About Us copy
- Any other brand communications

## Core Brand Voice

The Subtle Parts voice is:
- **Warm, unfussy, observant, and permissive**
- **Concise, precise, confident, and familiar** — like an artistic cousin you look up to
- **Never**: Lofty, academic, cheesy, salesy, or overly poetic
- **Grounded and craft-focused** — celebrating soulful design, texture, and originality
- **Sensory-rich and slightly poetic** — appealing to how things feel, look, and move

### Quick Voice Checklist

Before finalizing any copy, ask:
- ✓ Does it sound like talking to a stylish, creative friend?
- ✓ Does it emphasize sensory details (texture, color, feeling)?
- ✓ Is it concise and precise — does every word pull its weight?
- ✓ Does it feel authentic and grounded, not salesy?
- ✓ Does it celebrate the handmade nature without being lofty?

## Content Type Frameworks

### Product Descriptions

**Goal:** 2-3 sentences that are accurate, visual, textural, and SEO-friendly

**Process:**
1. Start with the jewelry itself — materials, shapes, textures, movement
2. Describe the feeling and function — how it wears, pairs, moves
3. Use warm, unfussy, sensory-rich language
4. Weave in SEO keywords naturally (materials, style descriptors, format)

**See:** [references/product-descriptions.md](references/product-descriptions.md) for detailed guidelines and vocabulary

### Email Newsletters

**Goal:** Evocative, concise copy that creates immediate immersion—vibe marketing, not explanation

**Approach: Vibe Marketing**
- **Less context-setting, more immediate immersion**
- Jump straight into the sensory experience
- Let materials, colors, textures do the talking
- Avoid over-explaining the "why" or setting up the scene
- Trust the audience to feel it

**Structure:**
1. **Open directly with the vibe** — Sensory imagery, bold aesthetic statement, or material-focused description
2. **Short, punchy product callouts** — Use sentence fragments for rhythm
3. **Brief CTA** — Simple invitation to shop

**Techniques:**
- Use alliteration: "Sunlit patinas, saltwater blues"
- Create contrasts: "Cool tones, warm skin"
- Deploy metaphors: "Like sunken treasures surfaced for summer"
- Employ incomplete sentences for impact: "Chandelier earrings. Necklaces that drape."
- Lead with materials and vibe, not features and benefits

**See:** [references/content-examples.md](references/content-examples.md) for newsletter examples and subject line patterns

### Instagram Posts

**Goal:** Personal storytelling that connects with the maker (Ashton) and tropical inspiration

**Approach:**
- More personal and conversational than other formats
- Connect to the making process, materials, or inspiration
- Use tropical and Miami/Yucatan imagery
- Can be playful and use emojis naturally
- Often shorter and punchier than email copy

### Ads

**Focus areas:**
- **Versatility and lifestyle fit** — day to night, casual to formal, gifting
- **Storytelling and inspiration** — tropics, handcrafted process, intentional living
- **The "why" behind the jewelry** — artistry, patience, value
- **Emotional language** — effortless style, bohemian elegance, understated charm, rustic beauty
- **Warm, inclusive tone** — conversational and welcoming

## Quick Reference: Brand Essentials

**Philosophy:** "Beauty isn't loud or precious—it's personal and wearable"

**Origin:** Miami and Yucatan-inspired, handcrafted from copper wire, simple tools, and fire

**Target Audience:** Women who embrace boho, eclectic, ethical fashion; value individuality, sustainability, and statement pieces

**Key Emotional Language:** Effortless style, bohemian elegance, understated charm, rustic beauty, tropical energy

**Materials:** Copper, natural stones, hand-shaped wire, oxidized patinas, weathered finishes

## Workflow

When writing copy for The Subtle Parts:

1. **Identify the content type** (product description, newsletter, Instagram, ad)
2. **Review the relevant reference file** for that content type
3. **Consider the target audience** — who are you speaking to?
4. **Draft using brand voice principles** — warm, unfussy, sensory-rich
5. **Check against the voice checklist** above
6. **Refine for conciseness** — does every word pull its weight?
7. **Ensure SEO keywords** are naturally woven in (for product descriptions)
